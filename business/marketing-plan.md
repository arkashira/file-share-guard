# Marketing Plan for File-Share-Guard
## Positioning
### Primary Positioning Statement
"Securely share files with confidence, without compromising on ease of use or control."

### Alternative Positioning Statements
1. "Empower teams to share files with peace of mind, using a self-hosted solution."
2. "Streamline secure file sharing with our lightweight, open-source solution."
3. "Take control of your file sharing, with automated link expiration and password protection."

## ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)
### Persona 1: Emily Chen, Marketing Manager at XYZ Corporation
- Daily Job: Managing marketing campaigns and coordinating with teams.
- Budget: $10,000 - $50,000 per year for software solutions.
- Pain Points: Securely sharing confidential marketing materials with external partners.

### Persona 2: David Lee, IT Manager at ABC Startup
- Daily Job: Overseeing IT infrastructure and ensuring data security.
- Budget: $5,000 - $20,000 per year for software solutions.
- Pain Points: Finding a lightweight, self-hosted file transfer solution that meets security requirements.

### Persona 3: Rachel Patel, Freelance Consultant
- Daily Job: Working with clients and sharing confidential project files.
- Budget: $1,000 - $5,000 per year for software solutions.
- Pain Points: Securely sharing files with clients without compromising on ease of use.

## Channels
### Top 3 Channels with CAC Estimate
1. **Content Marketing** (CAC: $50)
	* Blog posts, guides, and case studies on file sharing security.
	* Targeted SEO and social media promotion.
2. **Influencer Marketing** (CAC: $100)
	* Partner with industry influencers and thought leaders.
	* Sponsored content and product reviews.
3. **Paid Advertising** (CAC: $200)
	* Targeted Google Ads and LinkedIn Ads.
	* Focus on IT and marketing professionals.

## Content Cadence
### Week-by-Week Content Plan
Week 1-2: Introduction to file sharing security and the need for a self-hosted solution.
Week 3-4: Product features and benefits.
Week 5-6: Customer testimonials and success stories.
Week 7-8: Comparison with existing solutions and market analysis.
Week 9-10: Launch preparation and countdown.

## Launch Milestones
### D-30 (30 Days Before Launch)
- Finalize product features and documentation.
- Establish a content calendar and social media presence.

### D-0 (Launch Day)
- Launch the product and marketing campaign.
- Send out press releases and announce on social media.

### D+30 (30 Days After Launch)
- Analyze launch metrics and adjust the marketing strategy.
- Plan for future content and product updates.

### D+90 (90 Days After Launch)
- Review 90-day metrics and adjust the marketing strategy.
- Plan for future product development and expansion.

## Success Metrics
### DAU (Daily Active Users)
- Target: 100 DAU within the first 30 days.
- Metric: Track daily user engagement and activity.

### $MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue) Target
- Target: $10,000 MRR within the first 90 days.
- Metric: Track monthly revenue and subscription growth.
